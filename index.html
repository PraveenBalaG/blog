Top 10 Hill Station of India

Hill station of India

65 Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
Nestled in the Himalayas, Shimla offers a captivating blend of colonial history and natural beauty. The Mall Road bustles with shops and eateries, while the Viceregal Lodge showcases historical grandeur.
46 Darjeeling
Famous for its tea gardens and stunning vistas of the Kanchenjunga range, Darjeeling invites travelers to embrace a serene ambiance. The Toy Train, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, winds through the town's narrow streets.
42 Munnar, Kerala,
With its sprawling tea estates and biodiversity, Munnar, is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Eravikulam National Park is home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and offers breathtaking landscapes.
30 Ooty, Tamil Nadu
Known as the 'Queen of Hill Stations,' Ooty captivates with its botanical gardens and charming landscapes. The Government Rose Garden displays over 20,000 varieties of roses.
27 Leh-Ladakh, Jammu & Kashmir
Leh-Ladakh boasts stark yet mesmerizing landscapes and rich cultural heritage. The Shanti Stupa offers panoramic views of the region, and Pangong Lake's pristine blue waters leave visitors spellbound.
22 Manali, Himachal Pradesh
Nestled in the Beas River Valley, Manali is a haven for adventure seekers. Solang Valley offers thrilling activities like skiing and paragliding, while the Hadimba Devi Temple exhibits unique architecture.

Situated in Tamil Nadu’s Vellore district, Yelagiri is a serene hill station in Tamil Nadu that you must explore.

The Swamimalai Hill, rising to 4,388 feet, promises mesmerising panoramas during your trekking adventures. The district’s beautiful hills and valleys provide a visual feast for travellers, with the man-made Punganur Lake being one of the top attractions.

Coonoor is another famous hill station in Tamil Nadu nestled in the scenic Nilgiri Hills.

Sim’s Park, known for its exotic flora, provides a serene spot for a stroll, while the Lambs Rock viewpoint offers a mesmerising panorama of the surrounding hills and valleys. Coonoor’s year-round pleasant climate makes it one of the top hill stations in Tamilnadu that promises a refreshing retreat.

Kotagiri, perched at an elevation of 1,847 meters, is the third-highest hill station in the Nilgiri district of Tamil Nadu.

Here, you can enjoy strolls through the picturesque tea estates, breathe in the fresh mountain air, and savour the breathtaking views. Don’t miss the opportunity to visit attractions like Catherine Falls and Elk Falls, which offer natural beauty that will leave you spellbound. Kotagiri’s calm and pleasant climate, combined with its mesmerising charm, makes it one of the top hill stations in Tamilnadu.
